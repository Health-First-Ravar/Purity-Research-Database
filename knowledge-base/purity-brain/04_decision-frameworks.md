## PR & Sponsorship Scoring Framework

Standardized scorecard for evaluating all PR, sponsorship, event, and partnership
opportunities for Purity Coffee and Sacred Cups. **Score out of 100.**

### Thresholds

| Score | Decision |
|-------|----------|
| 80+ | Strong Yes |
| 70–79 | Likely Yes |
| 50–69 | Maybe (review with leadership) |
| Below 50 | Decline |

### Categories & Weights

| # | Category | Weight | What it measures |
|---|----------|--------|------------------|
| 1 | Audience Alignment | 25% | Avatar match, health/wellness context, spending power, practitioner density |
| 2 | Audience Reach & Engagement | 15% | Total audience size, engagement quality, platform authority |
| 3 | Brand Environment & Alignment | 20% | Premium positioning, sponsor/partner mix, message control, values alignment |
| 4 | Cost Efficiency | 15% | Total cash outlay, product cost, time investment, cost per qualified contact |
| 5 | Strategic & Long-Term Value | 15% | Relationship building, category authority, AI visibility impact, repeatability |
| 6 | Content & Amplification Potential | 10% | Content creation, cross-channel leverage, founder positioning |

### Automatic Disqualifiers (regardless of score)

- Direct competitor co-sponsors
- Pseudoscience platforms
- No message control
- Unsubstantiable claims required
- Cost over $10K without clear ROI path
- Exclusivity conflicts

### Brand Differences

- **Purity** targets biohackers, health-optimizers, functional medicine practitioners. Andrew leads.
- **Sacred Cups** targets therapists, healers, recovery communities, women in transformation. Amber leads.

### Calculation

Average criterion scores per category (1–10), multiply by weight, sum all categories for
total out of 100.

## Andrew's Email Voice & Style (CEO)

Authentic writing voice for use when drafting emails on Andrew's behalf.

- **Signature.** Always ends with "Best," (not "Best regards" or anything longer).
- **Length.** Generally concise. Not terse, not lengthy. Gets to the point without over-explaining.
- **Tone.** Professional but not cold. Warm without being effusive. No exclamation marks
  (or very rarely). Confident and direct.
- **Formatting.** Paragraphs naturally. Bullets appear occasionally but are not his default
  — more likely AI-influenced when they show up. Write in flowing prose unless content
  truly calls for a list.
- **Punctuation.** No em dashes. No hyphens in sentences. Clean punctuation overall.

### Vocabulary & Style Markers

- Thinks in business strategy and vision — uses phrases like "blue ocean," "powered by,"
  "Intel Inside" as metaphors.
- Frames things as opportunities, not problems.
- Uses "let's" to signal collaboration and forward motion.
- Asks questions genuinely — invites dialogue rather than dictating.
- References "we" often — inclusive, team-oriented framing.
- Comfortable being direct about what he thinks while still being open to other
  perspectives.
- Uses analogies to make big ideas concrete (e.g., "sell the pickaxes for the gold rush").

### What He Doesn't Do

- Doesn't hype or oversell.
- Doesn't hedge excessively.
- Doesn't use corporate filler language.
- Doesn't write in a way that feels stiff or formal.

### Same Style With Everyone

Andrew writes consistently whether emailing a vendor, a team member, or a leadership peer.
No code-switching in formality.

### Sample Phrases (authentic to his voice)

- "I think we have a big opportunity here..."
- "Let's set the direction and..."
- "Not innovating is a decision to keep small..."
- "If the wave is coming, I also want to sell the pickaxes..."
- "Let's you and I have the conversation first."
