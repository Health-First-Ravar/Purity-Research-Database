                                                                                         3.2. Docking of trigonelline in ˇ-amyloid(1–42) peptide

                                                                                             Docking procedure resulting in a trigonelline-A␤(1–42) com-
                                                                                         plex, showed that trigonelline may interact with most important
                                                                                         “key” histidine residues (His6, His13, His14) and one “key” tyro-
                                                                                         sine residue (Tyr10), present in the A␤(1–42) protein, altering its
                                                                                         structure and therefore inhibiting its aggregation. As shown in the
                                                                                         Fig. 2a and b, the trigonelline is mostly positioned close to the
                                                                                         imidazole ring of His13. In statistical terms, about 38% of all confor-
                                                                                         mations are located close to this residue. Due to the fact that His13
                                                                                         and His14 have some common conformational part, it should be
                                                                                         noted that 6% of these 38% positions of the system under studies,
                                                                                         impose with area of the His14. The His14 and Tyr10 are located
                                                                                         relatively close to each other, therefore it is difﬁcult to distinguish
                                                                                         the conformations of these residues separately. In their common
                                                                                         region (of His14 and Tyr10) about 30% of conformations are located.
                                                                                         The least populated space is located in the area of the His6. Rest
                                                                                         of the trigonelline conformations are located on the other side of
                                                                                         the A␤(1–42) helix, which is about 14%. Our results are in good
                                                                                         agreement with the results of studies on cotinine-A␤(1–42) com-
                                                                                         plex which were published by Echeverria and co-workers [18]. The
                                                                                         present study demonstrated that trigonelline (a similar structure to
                                                                                         the cotinine) has a relatively high binding afﬁnity to the A␤(1–42).
