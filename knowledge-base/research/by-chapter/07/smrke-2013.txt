                                                                                                                                         During coﬀee roasting major changes occur in coﬀee bean composition. Among others dark coloured
                                                                                                                                         melanoidins are formed, which are high molecular weight Maillard reaction products. A new approach
                                                                                                                                         is presented here to monitor the inﬂuence of roasting conditions on the antioxidant capacity of
                                                                                                                                         melanoidins and chlorogenic acids (CGAs) in a coﬀee brew. Validated Folin–Ciocalteu (FC) and ABTS
                                                                                                                                         assays were used as on-line antioxidant assays coupled (post-column) with high performance size-
                                                                                                                                         exclusion chromatography (HPSEC). HPSEC enabled the separation of melanoidins from CGAs and the
                                                                                                                                         determination of the antioxidant capacity of each fraction, within a total elution time of 25 min. Besides
                                                                                                                                         the on-line assay measurements, both assays were also applied oﬀ-line with ﬂow injection analysis (FIA).
                                                                                                                                         The maximum antioxidant capacity was determined to be at a light-to-medium roast degree, measured
                                                                                                                                         with both ABTS-FIA and FC-FIA assays as well as on-line ABTS assay. With FC on-line assay the maximum
                                                                                                                                         was found to be at a very light roast degree. Based on the peak areas obtained with the new coupled
                                                                                                                                         technique the roasting eﬀects on the variability of melanoidin and CGA contents in coﬀee brews were

                                                                                                                                         studied. The majority of melanoidins are already formed in the early stage of the roasting process and
                                                                                                                                         the relative contribution of melanoidins to the total antioxidant capacity increases towards darker

                                                                                                                                         roasts, mainly because CGAs degrade during roasting. A new parameter, the ratio of melanoidin to CGA
