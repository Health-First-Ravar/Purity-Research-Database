THE CORRELATION BETWEEN ACTIVE OXYGENS SCAVENGING AND 
ANTIOXIDATIVE EFFECTS OF FLAVONOIDS 
YUKIKO HANASAKI, SHUNJIRO OGAWA, and SHOZO FUKU! 
Department of Public Health Sciences, Kyoto Pharmaceutical University 5 Nakauchi-cho, 
Misasagi, Yamashina-ku, Kyoto, Japan 
(Received 21 July 1993; Revised 5 October 1993; Accepted 22 November 1993) 
Abstract--The abilities of 15 flavonoids as a scavenger of active oxygens (hydroxyl radical and superoxide anion) were 
studied, Hydroxyl radical ('OH) was generated by the Fenton system, and assayed by the determination of methanesulfinic acid 
(MSA) formed from the reaction of dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO) with "OH. (+)-Catechin, (-)-epicatechin, 7,8-dihydroxy flavone, 
and mtin showed the "OH scavenging effect 100-300 times superior to that of mannitol, a typical "OH scavenger. The other 
flavonoids showed no "OH scavenging effect at their concentrations up to 50 #M. Baicalein, quercetin, morin, and myricetin 
unexpectedly increased the "OH production in the Fenton system. The flavonoids tested now, except monohydroxy flavones, 
were more or less inhibitive to the superoxide anion (02) generation in the hypoxanthine-xanthine oxidase system. A great part 
of this inhibitory effect was likely owing to suppression of xanthine oxidase activity by the flavonoids. The flavonoids, which 
scavenged "OH or 02, were necessarily antioxidants to the peroxidation of methyl linoleate. However, there was a type of 
flavonoid such as morin, which have neither "OH nor 02 scavenging effect, but was a strong antioxidant. 
Keywords--Flavonoids, Hydroxyl radical, Scavenger, Lipid peroxidation, Free radicals 
INTRODUCTION 
Flavonoids are widespread in the plant kingdom. Its 
daily intake has been estimated to be 1 g, with the 
primary dietary source being vegetables) They are also 
present in many folk medicines. Some flavonoids have 
been reported to possess biological activities, e.g., anti- 
tumal, antibacterial, antiviral, inhibitory for enzymes, 
pro- or antimutagenic, and antioxidative properties. 2"3 
Some of these properties derive from the free radicals 
scavenging activities of flavonoids. Therefore, there 
are many reports relating to the reactivities of flavo- 
noids with active oxygen species. 4-8 It seems that the 
reactivities are varied with reaction conditions. For ex- 
ample, Husain et al.4 reported that 100 #M of myricetin 
and quercetin scavenge "OH generated by UV photoly- 
sis of hydrogen peroxide (H202), whereas Laughton et 
al. 6 reported these fiavonoids accelerate the production 
of "OH from H202 in the presence of Fe3-EDTA. Fur- 
ther, we could find few reports concerning the relation 
between antioxidative ability on lipid peroxidation and

Reagents

salt, hypoxanthine, and xanthine oxidase (grade I, from
