Caﬀeine promotes the expression of telomerase
reverse transcriptase to regulate cellular
senescence and aging
Li Tao, Wei Zhang, Yaxin Zhang, Mei Zhang, Yueying Zhang, Xing Niu, Qing Zhao,
Zhenxing Liu, Yuyin Li* and Aipo Diao
*
Telomere shortening is one of the main causes of cellular senescence. Caﬀeine is a natural stimulant
most commonly found in coﬀee and tea. In this study, caﬀeine was found to promote the expression of
telomerase reverse transcriptase (TERT) at both mRNA and protein levels, and consequently extended the
telomere length and prevented cellular senescence. Knockdown of TERT eliminated the eﬀect of caﬀeine
on telomere elongation. Moreover, animal studies indicated that caﬀeine promoted the expression of
TERT and extended the telomere length in the thymus and spleen of mice treated with caﬀeine for a long
period of eight months. In addition, caﬀeine restored the decline of organ index and improved the histo-
logical structural change of the thymus, spleen and liver of mice due to aging. These results suggest that
caﬀeine promotes the expression of TERT to delay cellular senescence and aging, which help to under-
stand the mechanism for the beneﬁcial eﬀects of caﬀeine containing foods on health.
1.
Introduction
Telomeres are nucleoprotein structures at the chromosome
ends, consisting of short tandem DNA repeats and associated
protective proteins.1 Telomeres protect genomic DNA from
end-to-end fusion and DNA degradation.2 Telomere shortening
with cell division consequently triggers cellular senescence.3
Cellular senescence is considered as the main cause of organ-
ism aging: senescence of stem cells leads to the depletion of
stem cells, which reduces tissue regeneration; moreover, senes-
cent cells secrete a variety of pro-inflammatory factors which
lead
to
chronic
inflammation
and
tissue
dysfunction.4
Telomerase is the only enzyme responsible for synthesizing
telomeric repeats at the ends of chromosomes and compensat-
ing for telomere shortening.5 Telomerase reverse transcriptase
(TERT) is the catalytic subunit and rate-limiting component of
the telomerase complex, and its expression level is correlated
with telomerase activity.6 Enhancing the expression of TERT
could decrease the aging process.7 Therefore, compounds pro-
moting TERT expression could be potential candidates for pre-
venting aging.
Coﬀee is one of the most frequently consumed beverages
worldwide. Human studies reveal that habitual coﬀee intake
reduces the all-cause mortality.8,9 Coﬀee consumption may
reduce the risk of type 2 diabetes mellitus and hypertension,
as well as other conditions associated with cardiovascular risk,
such as obesity and depression.10,11 Coﬀee is a complex bever-
age containing more than 1000 compounds, and caﬀeine is a
natural stimulant most commonly found in coﬀee.12 Natural
sources of caﬀeine include coﬀee beans, tea leaves, cocoa
beans and kola nuts.13 Caﬀeine extends the lifespan of
budding yeast and worms,14,15 aﬀects the health span of
organisms and elicits broad beneficial eﬀects.15–18 However,
the mechanisms and eﬀects of caﬀeine are not fully under-
stood with respect to aging or age-related diseases.
In the present study, a firefly luciferase reporter screening
system driven by a TERT gene promoter was established to
screen potential compounds promoting TERT expression, and
caﬀeine was identified and shown to promote TERT expression
and regulate cellular senescence and aging.

2.3.
Screening of compounds that promote the expression of
TERT
The activators of TERT expression were screened by using a
firefly luciferase reporter screening system driven by a TERT
gene promoter as in our previous work.19 In brief, HeLa cells
were plated in a 6-well plate and allowed to reach 60%–70%
confluence prior to transfection after 24 hours. Then the cells
were transfected with the reporter plasmid pGL2-TERT or the
control plasmid pGL2 using TurboFect transfection reagent
(Thermo scientific, Waltham, MA, USA). Next, the transfected
cells were plated onto a 96-well plate at a density of 8 × 103 per
well and treated with caﬀeine or other compounds including
sodium chloride, ethyl alcohol, vitamin C, vitamin E and zinc
sulfate for 36 hours. Finally, the luciferase activity was
measured using a multi-mode microplate reader (Biotek,
Winooski, VT, USA).

2.8.
Cellular senescence-associated β-galactosidase staining
Cellular senescence-associated β-galactosidase staining was per-
formed with a senescence β-galactosidase staining kit (Beyotime,
Shanghai, China) in accordance with the manufacturer’s instruc-
tions. Briefly, HEF cells were cultured for 10 passages with or
without caﬀeine treatment. When the cells reached approxi-
mately 90% confluence, they were fixed in a fixation solution for
15 minutes at room temperature, and then they were washed
three times with PBS for 3 minutes each time. Staining solution
(including 10 μL of staining buﬀer A, 10 μL of staining buﬀer B,
930 μL of staining buﬀer C, and 50 μL of X-Gal) was then added
to the cells and incubated at 37 °C overnight. The cells were
observed under a light microscope (Nikon, Tokyo, Japan).
2.9.
Animal experiments
Animal experiments were approved and performed in accord-
ance with the National and Institutional Guidelines for Animal
Care and Use. CD1 mice (6-week-old) were purchased from the
Laboratory Animal Center of academy of Military Medical
Sciences (Beijing, China quality certification number: SCXK
(Jun) 2012-0004). All mice were fed ad libitum and housed under
controlled
lighting
conditions
(12
light : 12
dark).
After
4 months of normal feeding, 10 male mice and 10 female mice
were randomly classified into two groups, each group consisting
of 5 male mice and 5 female mice. The aged + caﬀeine group
were treated with drinking water containing caﬀeine (5 mg per
kg body weight) every day for 8 months until they reached
14 months of age. Instead, the aged group was treated with
drinking water without caﬀeine for 8 months until they reached
14 months of age. Six-week-old CD1 mice were used as the
young mice control. At the end of the treatment, the mice were
fasted overnight. All mice were sacrificed and their body
weights were recorded. Subsequently, the thymus, spleen, liver,
kidneys, heart and lung of each mouse were carefully dissected
out, washed with physiological saline and weighed. The organ
index was calculated using the equation organ index = organ
weight (g)/body weight (g) × 100%. One portion of the thymus,
spleen or liver was snap-frozen in liquid nitrogen and stored at
−80 °C for western blot and telomere length analysis. The other
portion of the thymus, spleen or liver was fixed in 10% neutral
buﬀered formalin, embedded in paraﬃn, serially sectioned and
stained with hematoxylin–eosin (HE).
2.10.
Statistical analysis
Quantitative data were expressed as mean ± SD. Student’s
unpaired test was used to compare the diﬀerences between the
groups using SPSS v.25.0 (IBM, Armonk, NY, USA). P values
<0.05 were considered statistically significant.
3.
Results
3.1.
Caﬀeine promotes TERT promoter activity and
upregulates TERT expression
An established firefly luciferase reporter screening system
driven by TERT gene promoter was used to screen potential
compounds promoting TERT expression. Caﬀeine was identi-
fied to significantly increase TERT gene promoter activity,
but not other compounds including sodium chloride, ethyl
alcohol, vitamin C, vitamin E, and zinc sulfate (Fig. 1A).
Further analysis confirmed that caﬀeine induced-activation
of the TERT gene promoter occurred in a dose-dependent
manner, and 0.5, 1, 2 or 4 mM caﬀeine significantly pro-
moted the TERT promoter activity (Fig. 1B). RT-PCR was
used to detect the expression of TERT at the mRNA level and
the results showed that the mRNA of TERT increased follow-
ing caﬀeine treatment (Fig. 1C). Western blot was further
used to detect the protein expression of TERT and the
results showed that the TERT protein level was increased
after
caﬀeine
treatment
in
a
dose-dependent
manner
(Fig. 1D). These results indicate that caﬀeine promotes the
expression of TERT.
3.2.
Caﬀeine elongates the length of telomeres
To investigate the eﬀect of caﬀeine on the telomere length,
HeLa cells were treated with 1 mM caﬀeine for 10 passages,
and then their telomere lengths were assessed. Their telo-
mere lengths were increased following caﬀeine treatment
compared to the control (Fig. 2A). Furthermore, the loss-of-
function experiments were conducted to determine whether
caﬀeine induced elongation of telomere length by regulating
the expression of TERT. Since the expression of TERT is very
low or even absent in normal somatic cells,22,23 the two can-
cerous cell lines MCF-7 and HepG2 highly expressing telo-
merase were used for the depletion of TERT using shRNA
(shTERT). MCF-7 and HepG2 cell lines stably expressing
shTERT or the control scRNA were cultured for 40 passages,
and during the whole period of cultivation, MCF-7 or HepG2
cells were treated with 1 mM or 0.5 mM caﬀeine respectively,
and then the protein levels of TERT and the relative lengths
of telomeres in the cells were detected. Western blot analysis
showed an increased protein level of TERT following caﬀeine
treatment; in contrast, shRNA-mediated knockdown of TERT
eliminated the caﬀeine-induced expression of TERT (Fig. 2B).
Consistently, the length of telomeres was increased in MCF-7
or HepG2 cells with caﬀeine treatment; however, knockdown
of TERT eliminated the caﬀeine-induced telomere length
elongation (Fig. 2C). These results indicate that caﬀeine
extends the telomere length specifically by promoting the
expression of TERT.
3.3.
Caﬀeine reduces cellular senescence

can be characterized by stable cell cycle arrest. To determine
whether caﬀeine-induced expression of TERT is associated
with cellular senescence, flow cytometry was used to analyze
the cell cycle of HeLa cells treated with the minimum concen-
tration of caﬀeine (0.5 mM) for eﬃcient activation of the TERT
promoter (Fig. 1B). The results showed that the percentage of
cells in the S phase was 16.14% in the control cells, while the
percentage of cells entering the S phase increased to 24.1%
after treatment with 0.5 mM caﬀeine for 48 hours (Fig. 3A),

anti-aging agents, and several drugs have been reported to
promote telomerase activity, including cycloastragenol,25,26
curcuminoid,27 leptin28,29 and TA-65.30 However, the roles and
mechanism of healthy food in telomerase activity need to be
deeply explored. There have been several studies reporting the

caﬀeine promotes the expression of TERT, extends the telomere
length and prevents cellular senescence and aging, and
revealed the mechanism of caﬀeine activity which has not

It has been reported that caﬀeine intake induces an
increase in the circulating interleukin 6 (IL-6) levels and
results in signal transducer and activator of transcription 3
(STAT3) activation in the livers of mice.46 STAT3 regulates the
expression of TERT in both normal and tumor cells. STAT3
binds to the promoter region of the TERT gene, and its
depletion leads to the downregulation of TERT.47 In addition,
ER-stress-induced leptin resistance results in the decrease of
phosphorylation of STAT3, while caﬀeine markedly improves
the phosphorylation of STAT3.48 Further studies are needed
however
to
determine
whether
caﬀeine
promotes
TERT
expression via the STAT3 pathway.
Sirtuin 1 (SIRT1) is a NAD+-dependent deacetylase and is
implicated in anti-aging and maintenance of health. SIRT1
plays an important role in regulating telomere maintenance,
and increasing the activity of SIRT1 stabilizes telomeres and
mitigates telomere-dependent diseases.49,50 SIRT1 is a posi-
tive regulator of TERT and it regulates histone acetylation
and
methylation
at
the
TERT
gene
promoter.51
The
expression of SIRT1 is downregulated in both female52 and
male oﬀspring rats prenatally exposed to caﬀeine.53 During
the period of osteogenic diﬀerentiation, caﬀeine has bipha-
sic eﬀects: a caﬀeine concentration of 0.1 mM elevates SIRT1
expression
levels,
whereas
a
caﬀeine
concentration
of
0.3–1 mM downregulates SIRT1 expression levels.54 In this
study, the TERT gene promoter was not significantly acti-
vated under 0.25 mM caﬀeine treatment, however, it was sig-
nificantly increased with over 0.5 mM caﬀeine treatment and
in a dose-dependent manner (Fig. 1B). This suggests that the
eﬀect of caﬀeine on TERT expression and telomere length
may be
SIRT1-independent
in
some
kinds
of
cells or
animals, although whether caﬀeine aﬀects the expression of
SIRT1 in the cell and mice models used in this study needs
future investigation. In addition, several compounds have
been reported to maintain telomere homeostasis, and mag-
nesium, which is one of the main cellular ions, not only
plays an important role in the activity of telomerase, but also
regulates the telomere structure, integrity and function.55 It
will be interesting to find out whether magnesium has an
eﬀect on the caﬀeine-promoted expression of TERT and the
telomere length.
Since the activation of telomerase is also associated with
cancer, the relationship between caﬀeine consumption and
the risk of cancer need to be considered. However, according
to previous studies, caﬀeine intake has no eﬀect on increasing
the risk of ovarian and prostate cancers.56–58 Instead, caﬀeine
consumption is associated with reducing the risk of endo-
metrial and nonmelanoma skin cancers.56,59 A study on
Canadian woman shows that coﬀee and caﬀeine intake is not
associated with the overall risk of breast and ovarian cancers,
however, there is a tendency towards an increased risk of
breast cancer with increasing levels of coﬀee and caﬀeine
among premenopausal and normal weight women.56 Another
study on Swedish women indicated that caﬀeine intake is
negatively associated with the risk of overall and ER+/PR−
breast cancer.60
5.
Conclusion
We have identified and demonstrated that caﬀeine promotes
the expression of TERT, consequently increases the telomere
length, prevents cellular senescence and delays aging pheno-
types in mice. This study expands the understanding of the
bioactivity of caﬀeine in regulating cellular senescence and
aging, as well as the beneficial eﬀects of caﬀeine consumption
on health. Therefore, the eﬀect of caﬀeine containing foods on
delaying aging-associated phenotypes and improving health
merits needs further studies.
