 
Antioxidant potential of toraja robusta coffee 
extracts as a health supplement candidate 
Andi W. Fajriansyah Al-ghifari1* 
1Faculty of Health and Sport Sciences, Universitas Negeri Makassar, 90222 Makassar, Indonesia 
Abstract. Coffee is a beverage that is widely enjoyed by people all over the 
world. Its special aroma and taste, as well as its health benefits, have kept 
coffee popular to this day. Although coffee was discovered thousands of 
years ago, its potential, especially concerning its antioxidant content, has not 
been fully explored. The purpose of this study is to test the antioxidant 
strength of one of South Sulawesi's biodiversity treasures, Toraja robusta 
coffee extract, and to examine its relevance to sports health. The Toraja 
robusta coffee extract was brewed using hot water at a temperature of 92 °C. 
Subsequently, the antioxidant strength of the coffee extract was tested using 
the 2,2-Diphenyl-2-picrylhydrazyl (DPPH) IC50 method at a maximum 
wavelength of 516 nm. The results show that the antioxidant strength of 
Toraja robusta coffee is in the very strong category with an IC50 of 0.37 mg. 
Further research is needed to detect what compounds in Toraja robusta 
coffee act as antioxidants and to explore more deeply the potential of Toraja 
robusta coffee as an antioxidant supplement for athletes.  
Keywords: Antioxidant, DPPH IC50, Sports Health, Robusta coffee 
1 Introduction 
Coffee has become the most popular daily beverage worldwide. Its consumption trend 
has increased significantly in recent years [1]. This phenomenon is inseparable from the 
growing number of coffee shops in various places, leading to an increasingly widespread 
coffee-drinking culture. These coffee shops not only offer a place for coffee lovers to 
enjoy various types of coffee beans and brewing methods but also provide a comfortable 
space for students to complete assignments. 
Coffee not only provides economic benefits but is also capable of offering numerous 
health benefits. Several studies have found correlations between coffee consumption and 
enhanced physical performance [2], protection against health disorders such as 
Parkinson's disease, type 2 diabetes [3], and prevention of muscle damage [4]. 
Furthermore, coffee is also known to influence a reduction in mortality-causing diseases

[5]. However, one aspect that requires attention is ensuring that the daily consumption

With the various identified health benefits of coffee, it is known that the antioxidant 
content in coffee plays a significant role in its health advantages. Each type of coffee has 
a different antioxidant content, but in general, Robusta coffee has a higher antioxidant 
content than Arabica [7]. Robusta and Arabica are the most popular types of coffee in 
Indonesia [8]. Both types of coffee are widely cultivated in various regions of Indonesia, 
thus many areas are found to have their own distinctive coffee. 
Arabica coffee is a type suitable for cultivation in highlands with a minimum altitude 
of 800 meters above sea level (masl) and a temperature range of 16−20 °C [9]. If grown in 
lowland areas (below 600 masl), Arabica coffee's productivity is not optimal, and it is 
susceptible to coffee leaf rust disease or Hemileia vastatrix (HV), whereas Robusta coffee 
is the opposite of Arabica. Robusta coffee can be grown at lower altitudes, ranging from 
400 – 800 masl. One of the advantages of Robusta coffee is its resistance to leaf rust disease 
(HV), which commonly affects coffee plants in lowlands [10]. 
Similarly, the Toraja regency is geographically well-suited for coffee cultivation. 
Toraja regency is one of the regions in South Sulawesi known as a producer of the best 
coffee in Indonesia. The existence of Toraja Arabica coffee is recognized worldwide and 
has even earned the nickname "The queen of coffee". Likewise, Toraja Robusta coffee 
possesses advantages regarding its antioxidant content. However, both coffee types have 
their own respective connoisseurs. Therefore, this study aims to test the antioxidant 
content contained in Toraja Robusta coffee extract. 
2 Research Methods 
2.1 Research Location  
This research was conducted at the testing service unit of the Faculty of Pharmacy, 
Universitas Airlangga, Surabaya.  
2.2 Materials  
This study used Toraja Robusta coffee obtained from the Toraja Coffee Farmers 
Cooperative (PPKT), originating from the Rembon coffee plantation, Tanah Toraja. The 
coffee was roasted to a medium roast level and brewed using a manual brew method at a 
temperature of 92 °C. 
2.3 Extract Content Examination  
This research was conducted to determine the antioxidant strength of the Toraja Robusta 
coffee extract. The testing procedure for antioxidant strength was carried out referring to 
the standard procedures at the testing service unit of the Faculty of Pharmacy, Universitas 
Airlangga, Surabaya. The antioxidant strength results in this study refer to the research 
conducted by Molyneux [11].

To ensure the accuracy of the results, sample testing was continued in replication 2, 
as shown in Table 3. It was found that 0.3790 mg of the Toraja robusta coffee extract 
sample was able to counteract 0.53818 free radicals. 
Thus, it can be interpreted that the antioxidants contained in the Toraja robusta coffee 
extract are in the very strong category. According to Molyneux [11], the lower the IC50 
value, the stronger the antioxidant activity of the test sample. An IC50 value < 50 μg/ml 
indicates very strong antioxidant properties, 50 – 100 μg/ml is strong, 100 – 150 μg/ml is 
medium, and 150 – 200 μg/ml is weak. The findings of this study are in line with the 
research findings of Ajhar and Meilani that the antioxidant activity of the ethanol extract 
of Arabica coffee beans is categorized as very strong [12]. 
With these findings, it is hoped that a new dimension of knowledge can be added 
regarding one of Indonesia's biological treasures from Toraja, namely Toraja robusta

4 Conclusion 
The results of the antioxidant activity strength examination of Toraja robusta coffee 
showed an IC50 result of 0.37 mg. A low IC50 value indicates high effectiveness in 
scavenging free radicals. These findings provide scientific support for the potential of 
Toraja Robusta coffee as a natural source of antioxidants that can provide health benefits.
