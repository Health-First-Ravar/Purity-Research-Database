Chlorogenic Acid in Metabolic Diseases: From
Bench to Bedside
Yalin Lin, Zhiqiang Ke, Fei Cai
Abstract
Metabolic diseases encompass a range of chronic pathological conditions characterized by
disruptions in glucose, lipid, and protein metabolism. This category primarily includes obesity,
diabetes mellitus, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease, and metabolic syndrome, whose rising
global prevalence poses a substantial public health burden. Chlorogenic acid, a naturally
occurring phenolic compound widely found in coﬀee, tea, and medicinal plants, has recently
drawn considerable research interest due to its multifaceted pharmacological properties,
including the regulation of glucose and lipid metabolism, attenuation of oxidative stress, and
suppression of inﬂammatory responses. This review systematically summarizes the roles and
underlying mechanisms of chlorogenic acid in the management of various metabolic diseases.
Existing evidence indicates that chlorogenic acid exerts beneﬁcial eﬀects in ameliorating
obesity, diabetes, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease, metabolic syndrome, and hypertension.
The proposed mechanisms are primarily attributed to its antioxidant and anti-inﬂammatory
activities, modulation of gut microbiota composition, and protective eﬀects on target organs.

INTRODUCTION
The  prevalence  of  metabolic  diseases,  particularly  type  2  diabetes  mellitus,
obesity, and metabolic associated fatty liver disease (previously termed non-alcoholic
fatty liver disease, NAFLD), has been rising steadily worldwide, positioning them as
major threats to human health in the 21st century [1, 2]. These conditions not only
severely impair the quality of life of patients but also markedly increase the risk of
associated  complications,  imposing  a  substantial  economic  burden  on  global
healthcare systems. Current clinical management of metabolic diseases primarily
relies  on  lifestyle  interventions  and  pharmacotherapy.  Although  agents  such  as
glinides and sulfonylureas offer certain therapeutic benefits, their use is often limited
by pronounced side effects, modest efficacy, and poor long-term adherence  [3].
Consequently, the search for natural products with high efficacy, low toxicity, and

acid exhibits multiple biological activities, including antioxidant, anti-inflammatory,

application of chlorogenic acid, with the goal of offering insights to facilitate its

2.4 Modulation of gut microbiota
Chlorogenic acid alleviates metabolic diseases by modulating the gut microbiota
and regulating host immunity as well as intestinal barrier function  [32]. Studies
indicate that chlorogenic acid mitigates obesity by remodeling the gut microbiome
and serum metabolite profile, accompanied by reduced expression of genes involved
in hepatic lipid synthesis and lipid uptake [33]. It has been reported that a chlorogenic
acid complex improves metabolic diseases through modulation of the gut microbiota,
increasing  the  abundance  of  beneficial  bacteria  such  as  Faecalibacterium,
Akkermansia, and  Parabacteroides, while suppressing pathogenic genera including
Enterococcus and  Staphylococcus [34]. Zheng  and  colleagues  demonstrated  that
chlorogenic  acid  effectively  treats  metabolic  diseases  by  modulating  the  gut
microbiota, enhancing intestinal barrier function, suppressing toll like receptor 4-

3 Therapeutic effects  of chlorogenic acid  on  metabolic diseases and  related
conditions
Metabolic diseases generally refer to disorders arising from abnormalities in
glucose,  lipid,  and protein  metabolism,  with disease  risk influenced  by genetic,
environmental,  and  lifestyle  factors.  Common  types  include  diabetes  mellitus,
hypertension, obesity, and metabolic syndrome.
3.1 Chlorogenic acid and obesity
Obesity, a prevalent metabolic disorder, fundamentally results from a long-term
imbalance between energy intake and expenditure [39]. In a double-blind controlled
trial  involving  55  obese  individuals,  daily  administration  of  a  multicomponent
supplement containing chlorogenic acid over 12 weeks led to significant reductions in
body  weight  and  fat  mass,  indicating  a  therapeutic  effect  of  chlorogenic  acid-
containing supplements [40]. A randomized controlled trial reported that six months
of chlorogenic acid supplementation at 150 mg daily reduced body weight, blood
glucose, and lipid parameters, supporting its efficacy in treating obesity [41]. Another
study  found  that  12  weeks  of  coffee  consumption  containing  chlorogenic  acid
significantly decreased fat mass and body fat percentage, suggesting beneficial effects
on  metabolic  diseases  [42].  Additional  research  involving  obese  patients  taking
varying doses over eight months showed reductions in both body weight and blood

3.2 Chlorogenic acid and diabetes
Polyphenolic compounds such as chlorogenic acid have been reported to alleviate
type 2 diabetes [34]. One study involving patients with impaired glucose tolerance
found that 12 weeks of chlorogenic acid administration at 400 mg daily significantly
reduced fasting plasma glucose and insulin secretion, suggesting that chlorogenic acid
protects  pancreatic beta  cells  through antioxidant  and anti-inflammatory actions,
thereby exerting therapeutic effects on diabetes [44]. In a crossover study, Lestari and
colleagues  observed that three weeks  of coffee extract rich in chlorogenic acid
effectively lowered blood glucose and alleviated diabetes by protecting insulin target
organs  [45]. Johnston and colleagues reported that administration of 400 mL of
glucose  solution  (25  g  glucose)  with  caffeinated  or  decaffeinated  coffee—both
containing chlorogenic acid—reduced glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide
levels compared to water alone, indicating a decreased rate of intestinal glucose
absorption. This finding points to an anti-diabetic effect of chlorogenic acid [46]. In
summary, chlorogenic acid lowers blood glucose not through a single pathway but
through three synergistic mechanisms: protection of pancreatic beta cell function,

3.3 Chlorogenic acid and metabolic syndrome
Patti and colleagues found that metabolic syndrome patients receiving a natural
supplement  containing  chlorogenic  acid  for  four  months  showed  significant
reductions in body weight, body mass index, waist circumference, fasting blood
glucose, and total cholesterol, suggesting that chlorogenic acid alleviates metabolic
syndrome through regulation of glucose and lipid metabolism and energy expenditure
[47]. In a randomized clinical trial, patients with metabolic syndrome who took green
coffee bean extract for eight weeks experienced decreases in body weight and body
mass index (BMI), indicating that chlorogenic acid improves metabolic syndrome by
modulating fat metabolism  [48]. Collectively, these clinical studies confirm that
chlorogenic acid effectively improves body weight, BMI, waist circumference, fasting
glucose,  and total cholesterol  in metabolic syndrome patients,  with mechanisms
primarily involving regulation of glucose and lipid metabolism, promotion of energy
expenditure, and modulation of fat metabolism.

3.6 Chlorogenic acid and gout
   Chlorogenic acid reduces serum uric acid by inhibiting xanthine oxidase activity
without increasing urinary uric acid excretion, while also alleviating monosodium
urate crystal-induced paw swelling via suppression of interleukin-1β, interleukin-6,
and tumor necrosis factor-α. These dual actions—lowering uric acid production and
mitigating  inflammatory  responses—support  chlorogenic  acid  as  a  promising
candidate for further development against gouty arthritis  [56]. Future work should
validate these effects in clinical settings and clarify the compound’s bioavailability
and metabolism in the context of gout treatment.

5 CONCLUSION
First, further mechanistic studies are warranted. Chemical biology approaches
may be employed to identify the direct molecular targets of chlorogenic acid and to
elucidate its interaction patterns with target proteins, thereby providing a basis for
structural  optimization  and  expansion  of  indications.  In  one  study,  Wang  and
colleagues developed a bifunctional photoaffinity probe using chemical proteomics
and identified mitochondrial acetyl-CoA acetyltransferase 1 as a primary direct target
of chlorogenic acid  [60]. In another study, Xu and colleagues applied a clickable
photoaffinity probe technique to identify arabidopsis dehydroascorbate reductase 1 as
a covalent binding target of chlorogenic acid  [61]. Second, combination therapy
warrants investigation. The co-administration of metformin and chlorogenic acid has
been shown to synergistically suppress the expression of SREBP-1c (Sterol regulatory
element-binding protein 1c) and FAS (Fatty acid synthase) genes through enhanced
phosphorylation of AMPK and ACC (Acetyl-CoA carboxylase), resulting in greater
reduction of lipid accumulation compared to either agent alone [6]. Thus, developing
combination regimens  based on the synergistic effects of chlorogenic acid with
existing drugs such as metformin may offer advantages in terms of enhanced efficacy
and  reduced  toxicity.  Third,  novel  drug  delivery  systems  should  be  explored.
Advanced  formulation  technologies,  including  nanoparticles,  liposomes,  and
