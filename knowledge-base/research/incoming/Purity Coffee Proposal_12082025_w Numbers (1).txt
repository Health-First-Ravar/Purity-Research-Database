Prepared by Dr. Gabriel Keith Harris 
Department of Food, Bioprocessing, and Nutrition Sciences at NC State University 
The purpose of this one-page proposal is to outline our capacity for chemical analyses and other 
opportunities for collaboration. 
Chemical Analyses 
1. Ca*eine, CGA, Trigonelline, N-methylpyridinium, 5-hydroxymethylfurfural: Phenolics
present in both green and roasted coQees.  Can be analyzed using similar HPLC methods.
2. Phenylindanes: Bitter phenolics resulting from CGA degradation.  Would requires an
LC/MS for detection.
3. Flavonoids: Phenolics present at lower concentrations than caQeine or CGAs. Primarily
catechins and quercetin derivatives. Total ﬂavonoids can be measured by
spectrophotometry.  Individual ﬂavonoids must be measured by HPLC.
4. Diterpenes:  Lipid compounds are present in both green and roasted coQee.  They require
a separate type of HPLC analysis.
5. Biogenic Amines: Naturally present in coQee, especially lower quality coQees.  Some
destroyed by roasting, others are produced by roasting.  All can be analyzed by HPLC.
6. Total Phenolics: A measure of a wide range of phenolics, based on their reducing capacity.
Spectrophotometer analysis.
