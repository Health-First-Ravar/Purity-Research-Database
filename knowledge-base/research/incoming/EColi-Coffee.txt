Summary 
Here we report that coffee showed a strong antibacterial action on Escherichia
coli (E. coli) and Helicobacter pylon (H. pylon). This action against H. pylon suggests that
coffee may have action as a useful natural inhibitor of gastritis and gastric ulcers.
Key Words: coffee, antibacterial effects, Escherichia coli, Helicobacter pylori
Introduction
Coffee attracts attention as not only a table luxury
but also a food with variety of side effects, both ben-
eficial and harmful. For example, Tam et al. [1]
found that coffee has antibacterial effects. This sug-
gests that coffee may be useful as a natural inhibitor
against bacteria. Here we also show that coffee offers
to antibacterial action against two important intesti-
nal microorganisms, Escherichia coli (E. coli) and
Helicob acter pylori (H pylori).

E. coli: Survival by roasted coffee in the solid
medium 
decreased as coffee concentration 
was

two times the concentration to obtain the effect as

On the other hand, coffee is also reported to act as

antioxidant molecules mostly derived from dietary
fruits or vegetables such as coffee as well as enzymes
that scavenge ROS [8-10]. To learn whether the
cytotoxicity to bacteria results from ROS generation
by coffee, we used Caenorhabditis elegans (C. elegans),
which has received much attention as a model to dis-
sect the genetics [11]. The mev-1 mutant is an opti-
mal experimental mutant to study oxidative stress,
because it shows hypersensitivity to oxygen [12-17].
As there is no difference between wild type and the
mev-1 mutant in medium including coffee under
hyperoxia (data not shown), it is unlikely that oxida-
tive stress mediate the deleterious effects including
antibacterial effect of coffee.
In conclusion, coffee inhibits the growth of bacte-
ria, E. cofi and H. pylori. The antibacterial effect of
coffee, especially against H pylori, may contribute to
preventing gastritis, digestive gastric ulcer or gastric
cancer of which H pylori could play an important
role in the episode [18, 19], even if coffee does not
offer to oxidant action.
