Does Purity test for ochratoxin A, and what is the typical level?
Yes. Every Purity blend is third-party tested for ochratoxin A through an ISO 17025 accredited laboratory. Results across the PROTECT, FLOW, EASE, and CALM blends consistently land below the detection limit of the assay (typically under 0.5 ppb), well beneath the EU regulatory threshold of 5 ppb for roasted coffee.

OTA formation is driven by Aspergillus ochraceus during drying and storage, so green sourcing and storage integrity are the primary controls. Roasting reduces residual OTA by roughly 70 to 80 percent but is not the gatekeeper.

Is the PROTECT blend lower in acrylamide than a dark roast?
Acrylamide in coffee follows a roast curve that peaks around medium roast (approximately 220 to 230 °C) and then decreases as the roast progresses darker. It is not a monotonic light to dark reduction.

PROTECT is developed on a lighter profile optimized for chlorogenic acid retention, which typically places it slightly below the medium-roast peak but above very dark roasts on acrylamide. The tradeoff is real: lighter roasts retain more chlorogenic acids and intact trigonelline. Darker roasts carry more melanoidins and NMP, and less acrylamide. No single roast is universally healthiest. The best choice depends on the target health outcome.

Across specialty-grade roasted coffee, acrylamide levels sit well below daily-intake exposures of concern in EFSA modelling.
